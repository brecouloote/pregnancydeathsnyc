Maternal Death Rates Across New York City Boroughs

By Breona Couloote
December 11, 2020

Photo credit: Pixabay

Back in April, Amber Rose Issac was a mother to be who tweeted about her horrendous experience dealing with hospital staff at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx. Two hours later, Issac passed away due to complications with HELLP syndrome.

According to NYC Health, the Bronx ranks the highest in pregnancy-related mortality ratios out of all five boroughs with 26.9 deaths per 100,000 live births.

Bronx (Re)Birth was formed by three black Bronx doulas to help Bronx POC families so they never have to go through what Issac did. Carmen Mojica, midwife and co-founder of Bronx (Re) Birth said doula care can lead parents to other education opportunities.

“Being doulas in the Bronx and advocating for more women in the Bronx learn not just about doulas, but I feel like doulas are a gateway into like childbirth education,” she stated.
